# Wetlands as Natural Infrastructure

## Flood regulation

Wetlands temporarily store rainfall and river overflow. Their vegetation and shallow basins slow runoff, lower downstream flood peaks, and reduce erosion. Restoring floodplain wetlands can therefore complement levees and drainage infrastructure.

## Climate role

Wetland plants capture atmospheric carbon, while waterlogged soils can preserve organic matter for long periods. Peatlands hold especially large carbon stores. Draining wetlands exposes this material to oxygen and can release carbon dioxide, so protection avoids emissions as well as maintaining future sequestration.

## Limits

Benefits depend on wetland type and condition. Some wetlands emit methane, and poorly located restoration cannot eliminate all flood risk. Decisions should consider the full greenhouse-gas balance and watershed context.
